COVID-19 Cases Surge – DOH Junks Mandatory Face Mask Rule

Despite the recent surge of COVID cases across the country, the Department of Health (DOH) in coordination with the Inter-Agency Task Force (IATF) have voiced their opposition to the return of mandatory face mask rules on Tuesday.

President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. has previously suggested that the government may reinstate mandatory face mask rules in line with recommendations from the DOH and IATF as the number of cases nationwide continue to increase.

“Hindi po kailangan na back and forth ang policies natin, kailangan po lahat tayo ay magtulong-tulong para tayo ay makaalpas sa ganitong sitwasyon,” quipped DOH officer-in-charge Maria Rosario Vergeire.


Higher and higher

According to the DOH, there were 4,456 new infections in the past week. A 42% increase from the rates logged from April 17 to 23.

However, Vergeire has stressed that the current influx of instances does not equate to more hospital admissions and severe and critical cases.

“Itong pagtaas ng kaso hindi nag translate sa mas maraming hospital admissions, and hindi rin po siya nag translate sa mas maraming severe and critical cases and even deaths,” said the DOH officer-in-charge.


Shifting mindsets

The official had also said that the people must shift their mindset about increasing COVID-19 reports.

“No matter how high the positivity rate is, it does not give an accurate picture of our COVID-19 situation. Most are using antigen tests. Some are not having any tests at all, but are just isolating themselves when they are sick,” said Vergeire.

In line with this, she also shared how the country’s health utilization rate remains at low risk due to our wall of immunity brought by vaccinations and how the public must adapt to life with the virus.

“Tataas bababa ang mga kaso dito sa ating bansa dahil nandito ang virus, hindi mawawala and the virus will mutate and produce variants every now and then,” she added.


Current state

The Philippines added 1,190 new COVID-19 cases on Thursday, bringing the active tally to 8,371 and making it the highest number of fresh cases over the past three days.

Meanwhile in the past 14 days, the National Capital Region checked in the highest number of new cases with 4,172. It was followed by Calabarzon with 2,038, Central Luzon with 583, Western Visayas with 505, and Davao Region with 374.

Vergeire explained that what the masses need is for people to know how to protect themselves and their family from the disease by choosing to wear masks when going to high-risk places.

The DOH has so far logged a total of 4,093,421 cases, with 4,021,102 recoveries and 66,444 deaths.
